Gamma on the Nintendo DS

Discussion

On the Nintendo DS screens, the gamma ramp is set to a 2.0 curve. PC monitors use a 2.5 curve. This means that if a movie looks great on a PC, you need to adjust it slightly for good looking playback on the DS. Otherwise, the video will appear to be washed out. Although the DS screens always seem to be pretty dark, so sometimes being washed out is actually ok - it depends on your video.

If you are compressing CGI scenes, then the best way to handle this is just to set the gamma to 2.0 in your rendering options. It will look a little dark on your PC monitor, but will look great on a TV. If you are capturing video, check out the documentation on the capture software to find the gamma control options.

If you can't re-generate original video directly at 2.0, then you can use Bink's built-in gamma adjuster. Just set the gamma option in the Bink compression window to 0.8 to convert from PC 2.5 gamma video to LCD 2.0 gamma.
