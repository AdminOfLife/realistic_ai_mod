HDTV Video on the Xbox

Discussion

If you use Bink's new register combiner method of video playback, even the slow-ish CPU in the Xbox can play gorgeous 1280x720 HDTV video. This will let your game cut-scenes look much,much better than DVD when played on HDTV displays!

To do this, compress your movies at a resolution of 1280x720, with a data rate of around 1,500,000 bytes per second. This will give you great looking video that the Xbox will play fine.

If your game is 640x480 only, then when playing high-res video, you can either change the display mode, or simply scale the videos down at run-time (they will still look better than original 640x480 movies).

Now that the register combiner method is so easy to build into your game, we recommend all future Xbox games use high-def video - it's a great way to take advantage of the Xbox's terrific 3D hardware.
