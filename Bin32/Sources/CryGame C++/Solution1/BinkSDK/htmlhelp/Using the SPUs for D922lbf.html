Using the SPUs for Decompression on the Sony PS3

Discussion

Bink can do all of its decompression on the SPUs. This is nice because it leaves the overworked PPU completely free for other things. For 720p video, Bink needs one SPU, but if you want to decompress even faster, then you can give it up to two per video. You can even decompress multiple Bink videos by spreading the work over additional SPUs. At the limit, you can decompress ten 1280x720 videos or thirty-six 640x480 videos at once on the PS3.

Rather that supply a pre-built ELF for the SPU decompression module, we instead supply static libraries that you can use to build this ELF. This technique lets us supply multiple SPU support libraries, so that you aren't locked into any particular SPU image format.

So, to make an SPU ELF, you take the Bink SPU library (BinkSPU.a) and link it with an SPU support library (like BinkSPU_sputhreads.a, BinkSPU_raw.a, or BinkSPU_spurs.a) to create an ELF that you load with the BinkStartAsyncThread function. Then, you link your PPU application with the matching PPU library (like BinkPPU_sputhreads.a, BinkPPU_raw.a, or BinkPPU_spurs.a). So, there are always two halves to the SPU support - one for the SPU and one for the PPU. One you link with BinkSPU.a to make an ELF, and one you link with your PPU app to talk to the other SPU half.

Right now, we ship with SPU thread support, RAW support and SPURS task support. You can also write your own SPU support library as well. To do this, you need to implement the small APIs that are prototyped in the radspu.h and radppu.h header files.
