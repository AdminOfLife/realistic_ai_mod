HDTV Video on the Xbox 360

Discussion

Using Bink's pixel shader method of video playback, the Xbox 360 can easily play gorgeous 1280x720p or 1920x1080i video. Microsoft has required that all cutscenes on the Xbox 360 should be at least 1280x720, so Bink has you covered. This will let your game cut-scenes look much, much better than DVD when played on HDTV displays!

To do this, compress your movies at a resolution of 1280x720, with a data rate of around 1,500,000 bytes per second. This will give you great looking video that the Xbox 360 will play without breaking a sweat. For 1920x1080i video, create the movie as a 1920x540 movie at 59.94 Hz, using a 1,800,000 bytes per second data rate.

Video on the Xbox 360 will really look amazing - Microsoft is really pushing the high-def capabilities of the console, and we're excited to see what you all come up with!
